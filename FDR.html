Fun Facts

Franklin Delano Roosevelt, often referred to by his initials FDR, was an American politician who served as the 32nd president of the United States from 1933 until his death in 1945. He won a record four presidential elections and became a central figure in world events during the first half of the 20th century. Roosevelt directed the federal government during most of the Great Depression, implementing his New Deal domestic agenda in response to the worst economic crisis in U.S. history. He led America through World War II, and died in office during his fourth term.
  • He is the only president to serve more than two terms
  • He is distantly related to 11 other presidents
  • He won all of his elections in landslides, winning at least 53% of the popular vote every time
  • When he was 39, he got a disease called polio, and was paralyzed from the waist down for the rest of his life
  • Although he was handicapped, he kept it largely a secret from the public because he thought it would make him look weak
  • He was the first president to fly in a plane while in office
